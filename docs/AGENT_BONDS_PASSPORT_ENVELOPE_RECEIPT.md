# Agent Bonds Passport, Envelope, and Receipt

## Passport

The Agent Bond Passport is the portable identity, capability, capacity, and reputation record for an agent.

It answers:
- who the agent is;
- who operates it;
- what task classes it supports;
- what capacity it can carry;
- what receipts back its history;
- what A2A, MCP, and x402 surfaces it exposes.

## Envelope

The Bonded Task Envelope is the standardized promise made between requester, agent, verifier, and settlement path.

It fixes:
- task class;
- payout and required bonds;
- policy and windows;
- evidence schema;
- acceptance criteria;
- recourse rules;
- payment-funding mode.

## Receipt

The Bonded Execution Receipt is the durable terminal record of a bonded task.

It records:
- the promised envelope;
- the liable participants;
- evidence and verifier result;
- settlement amounts;
- reputation deltas;
- integration references;
- FlowPulse linkage.

These three primitives are the minimum foundation for any later credit, underwriter, A2A, MCP, x402, or public-claim layer.
